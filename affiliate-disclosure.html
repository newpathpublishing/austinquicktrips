Affiliate Disclosure

FTC-Compliant Disclosure Statement

What This Disclosure Means

Austin Quick Trips participates in affiliate marketing programs. This means when you click on certain links on our website and make a purchase, Austin Quick Trips may earn a commission from the seller at no extra cost to you.

This is a common and transparent way that many content websites sustain their operations. The affiliate commissions help us continue creating free travel guides, reviews, and recommendations for Austin area travelers.

Our Affiliate Programs

Austin Quick Trips has affiliate relationships with the following companies:

Our Affiliate Partners

  • Viator: Tours, activities, and experiences
  • Booking.com: Hotels, accommodations, and vacation rentals
  • Amazon Associates: Books, gear, and travel products
  • GetYourGuide: Tours, attractions, and activities
  • TripAdvisor: Attractions, reviews, and travel information

How We Mark Affiliate Links

We make every effort to clearly mark affiliate links so you know when a link may result in a commission for Austin Quick Trips. You may see:

  • Text labels such as "affiliate link," "sponsored," or "ad"
  • HTML attributes like "rel=sponsored" or "rel=nofollow" (visible in technical browser inspection)
  • Affiliate disclosure statements near the links

If you ever see a link on our site that should be marked as an affiliate link but isn't, please contact us and let us know.

We Only Recommend What We Believe In

This is important: we do not make recommendations based on commission rates. We recommend places, products, and services because we genuinely believe they offer good value and will enhance your travel experience around Austin.

We have turned down affiliate partnerships and will not recommend products or services if we don't believe in them, regardless of commission potential. Your trust is more valuable to us than any commission.

No Extra Cost to You

You will not pay more because you use our affiliate links. In fact, sometimes you may get special discounts through our partnerships. The commission comes from the merchant and does not affect your final price.

You are free to visit these companies directly if you prefer, but we appreciate it when you use our links as a way to support our work.

What We Use Commissions For

Affiliate commissions help us:

  • Keep Austin Quick Trips free and ad-free (we don't sell your data or show invasive ads)
  • Create new, original travel guides and recommendations
  • Keep our content updated with current information
  • Invest in website infrastructure and security
  • Support our team who researches and tests recommendations

FTC Compliance Statement

Austin Quick Trips is committed to complying with the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) guidelines on endorsements and testimonials. In accordance with 16 CFR Part 255, we disclose any material connections between Austin Quick Trips and companies whose products or services are promoted on our site.
