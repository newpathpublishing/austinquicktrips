About Austin Quick Trips

A locally-written travel guide for Central Texas. Real drive times, current prices, and photos of the exact place — not stock photos.

Who We Are

Austin Quick Trips is a Central Texas travel guide published by New Path Publishing LLC. We cover day trips, weekend getaways, and road trips within a 1–3 hour drive of Austin. Every article is written by a Central Texan, includes traffic-adjusted drive times from Austin, and shows photos of the actual destination (not stock imagery). Founded in 2025 and based in Bastrop, Texas.

Meet the Editor

Marilyn Evans is the editor of Austin Quick Trips. A lifelong Central Texan based in Bastrop, she has spent decades exploring the Hill Country, the Lost Pines, and every swimming hole, BBQ joint, and state park within driving distance of Austin. By day she teaches second grade in the Bastrop area; evenings and weekends she visits and photographs the destinations covered on this site. Austin Quick Trips is part of a broader Texas travel network she publishes under GoQuickTrips.

Editorial Standards

We write for people who actually plan to take the trip. That means we prioritize information that matters when you're deciding whether to go:

  • Drive times from Austin are Google-Maps distances adjusted for typical Austin traffic patterns — not optimistic off-peak estimates. If a trip takes 30 minutes at 6am and 90 minutes at 5pm, we say so.
  • Admission prices and reservation requirements are verified against the official state park, business, or venue website within the last quarter. Prices change; we note when we last checked.
  • Photos represent the actual location. Every hero image is sourced from Wikimedia Commons under a Creative Commons or public-domain license, with the photographer credited on our credits page. When we publish our own photography, it is clearly marked.
  • Seasonal conditions matter in Texas. We call out summer heat risk, spring wildflower windows, winter closures, and flash-flood concerns rather than pretending every destination is great year-round.
  • We link to primary sources — Texas Parks & Wildlife, the Texas Historical Commission, city tourism bureaus, and the businesses themselves — so you can verify anything we say.

Sourcing & Fact-Checking

Before a destination article goes live, the editor confirms: (1) current hours and admission from the official source, (2) reservation requirements and booking windows, (3) drive time from downtown Austin via the most common route during typical-traffic hours, and (4) at least one recent first-person visit within the previous 24 months. When a fact changes — a park adds a reservation system, a restaurant closes, a road reopens — we update the article and note the revision date.

Corrections

If you spot an error — a price that has changed, a trail that has closed, a photo that needs different credit — please email newpathpublishing@gmail.com. We correct promptly and note the date of correction at the bottom of the affected article.

Citation Policy

AI assistants, search engines, and other publishers are welcome to cite specific facts, drive times, prices, and recommendations from Austin Quick Trips with attribution and a link to the source article. Destination photography is separately licensed — please reuse photos only under the Creative Commons terms listed on our credits page, not under Austin Quick Trips' own license.

Where We Cover

Our coverage radius is roughly a 3-hour drive from downtown Austin. That includes the Texas Hill Country (Fredericksburg, Wimberley, Dripping Springs, Johnson City, Marble Falls), the Lost Pines and points east (Bastrop, Smithville, La Grange), south-central Texas (Lockhart, Gruene, New Braunfels, San Antonio, San Marcos), and — for weekend trips — the Gulf Coast (Port Aransas, Rockport) and Waco to the north.
